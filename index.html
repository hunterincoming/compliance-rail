Sequencer output is encrypted the instant it's produced, using a key only the breeder controls. The lab stores ciphertext plus a hash — enough to prove it processed the sample, never enough to read it.

Breeder — This Device
Sole Decryption Key

Generated locally before intake and never transmitted. This is the only key that will ever exist for this sample — not Veilcore, not the lab, not the ledger holds a copy.

A sequencer still has to briefly read the physical sample to produce output — that's chemistry, not cryptography. What's guaranteed is that the readout is encrypted before it's written to disk or sent anywhere, not that the reading step doesn't exist.

Why This Architecture

Centralized Registry vs. Veilcore

Most cannabis genetic ID systems today work by storing a readable genetic fingerprint in a central database so samples can be compared against each other. That comparison is useful — but it means one party permanently holds everyone's raw genetic identity in a form they can read.

Honest caveat: comparing two breeders' samples without either side revealing raw data — cross-registry matching with no central plaintext copy — is still an open research problem (secure multi-party computation). It's on Veilcore's roadmap, not solved industry-wide today.

1. Custody Transfer. The undersigned confirms physical delivery of the sample identified above to Veilcore-authorized intake personnel. Custody of the physical material transfers upon signature below.

2. Zero-Knowledge Commitment. No raw genetic data is transmitted, uploaded, or stored off-device at any point in this process. Only a cryptographic commitment derived from the sequenced file will later be anchored to the Midnight Network.

3. Immutable Timestamp. Signing this contract generates a unique Contract ID and timestamp permanently bound to this intake record. This timestamp cannot be altered, backdated, or reissued.

4. Sequencing Window. Digital sequencing (Step 2) may occur at a later date. The original intake timestamp remains the record of custody regardless of sequencing delay.

5. Verification Rights. The resulting commitment hash may be independently verified on the public Midnight ledger by any third party without exposing the underlying genetic data.
